Challenged, burdened, and crushed.

I am the eldest grandchild on my mother's side and the 24th grandchild on my father's.

As I grew, life has challenged my limits: family challenges my endurance, school challenges my knowledge, friends challenge my creativity, and love challenges my patience.

No matter the challenge. Failure is not a choice or result; it is a warning I heed.

-My endurance, even when exhausted, I still push on.

-My knowledge, while adequate, I still learn.

-My creativity, while impressive, I still improving.

-My patience, while idle, I still hope.

As I grew, expectations were placed on my shoulder to be successful.

"Do well in school."

"Get a great job."

"We want you to be successful."

I know I am not perfect; no one is. So instead of achieving perfection, I choose to hone my planning skills. After all, while everyone tries to swim against the river, I am at the side walking along the banks.

Thinking smart has helped me accomplish some achievements in my life.

1. Get first in a science competition.

2. Be part of my school's national debate team.

3. Join theater arts.

4. Shortlisted for Young Writers Program: Open Mic Night

5. Had a girlfriend

6. Publish some story (Walf_Albot (@Walf_Albot) - Wattpad)

7. Started a YT Channel (@Wal_Were)

I am a simple person. If my parents want me to succeed, then I will. Anything else is a personal goal of mine.

• Get married one day

• Have kids

• Have enough money so I can spend it leisurely

• On progress




Guess that is all I need to share. Remember, think smart, not hard.
